howdy! i'm lehana.

Thanks for checking out my website! My name is Lehana, and I am a senior electrical engineering student at Texas A&M University, with minors in math and technical sales.


My journey in engineering started in my junior year of high school, where I took AP Chemistry- the first science class I truly enjoyed. Entering A&M's freshman general engineering program, I had the opportunity to explore a ton of different disciplines and identify where my interests specifically aligned. With a few tweaks to my career goals here and there, I finally landed on electrical engineering!


I’m currently drawn to radio frequency wireless communications and analog systems within electrical engineering. The idea of connecting people through signals that we can't even see is something I find deeply fascinating. I hope to contribute to the design and development of the technologies that make global connectivity possible. Looking ahead to my post-graduate career, I’m excited by roles that allow me to engage with people from diverse backgrounds and perspectives. I believe that there is immense value in learning from others, and I want to spend my 20s expanding both my technical knowledge and my understanding of the world. All of this has led me to explore applications engineering, where I can combine technical experience with customer engagement.


Over the past three years, I have had the opportunity to deeply engage with my campus, whether it be through my work experience, leadership and involvement in my student organizations, or by partipating in aggie traditions. I absolutely love Texas A&M and how engrained tradition is into the culture of the college, and I actively try to find ways to get involved. You can learn more about my involvement here. WHOOP!


Outside of school and work, you can usually find me outdoors with an iced coffee in hand—reading, spending time with friends, or people-watching. I love finding creative ways to express my personality, and enjoy picking up random hobbies and side quests.




about kaju coffeehouse

When I was first trying to come up with how I wanted to brand myself, I was overwhelmed with choices. Do I include my resume on the main page or on its own page? Do I go for the minimalist aesthetic or do I paragraph everything that I have ever done in my entire life? Wait...I don't even know how to use HTML... AKDFGHSDHFKJSHDFKDH


It felt like everyone around me had their entire life figured out and presented neatly on a website. Tons of internships and side projects they loved, research experience and publications from even before they hit puberty, AND a perfectly crisp website that could tell a viewer everything about them with one click. Whereas me? I didn't even know how to change the font from Arial to something less corporate. Finishing up the second semester of my junior year (aka the most difficult semester of my life), I was lost, feeling inadequate in my ability to design a website, in the major I had spent the last three years in, and in the choices I had made up to that point in life.


Until I realized that it truly was not that deep.


The people who had it figured out didn't actually have it figured out. They just took the first step to learn something that wasn't necessarily on the course syllabus. They were okay making the mistakes, accepting the consequences, and learning from them, as opposed to dodging the topics and learning that did not benefit them in the short term. Though it could sound like an easy task for some, creating a website purely using HTML, CSS, and Javascript was something that terrified me, because I had no experience in it. Coming from a very rocky background in coding, the idea of opening VScode for ANYTHING made my brain want to turn off. But I also wanted the cute website that everybody had, and the internal accomplishment that I had done something difficult and out of my comfort zone. So, I had two options. I could either spend the summer before the start of my senior year passively doing the work that was assigned to me, ignoring opportunities to learn beyond the bare minimum necessary to graduate and get a job, and keep questioning why I felt so unsatisfied with where I was at. Or, I could approach every problem with a growth mindset. Instead of being scared of failing, or what people thought of me, I would just have to...start.


So, that brings me to this website. The thing that I wanted to create for so long, but never made the time to actually learn and implement. Did it take me five times the amount of time it would take a UI/UX student to create this website? Did I watch hours of random tutorial videos and consult chatgpt on half of what I wanted to do? Absolutely. But at least now I can say that I started, grinded, and saw a project the full way through, learning a skill I thought I would never have in the process. I hope to use this website as a springboard for my academic, professional, and personal goals, and record my journey in self development. You don't have to know everything about something to be curious. You just have to start somewhere.


As for the personal brand? I was overthinking it. Anyone who knows me knows my undying love for coffee (and my overambitious dream of opening a coffee shop/flower shop/bookstore/bakery/planetarium). Kaju is the hindi word for cashew, and, in the context of this website, is short for my favorite indian sweet, kaju barfi. My personal brand should be about me, so why not center it around two things I love?


Welcome to kaju coffeehouse. I hope you enjoy exploring this site, and i'm going to try and keep it as real as possible as I grow both this website and my life, this summer and onwards. <3
